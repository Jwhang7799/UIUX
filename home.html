Understanding User Interactions
with a Water Sprinkler Controller
What is a Water Sprinkler Controller?

The water sprinkler controller allows any user to set two recurring schedules to water their lawn and includes various features such as timers to control water usage, three program start times to water multiple times in a single day, and a manual start button to start the sprinklers immediately. This helps users never forget to turn the sprinklers on and allows them to do so at optimal times to prevent fungus from growing.

How Intuitive are Water Sprinkler Controllers?
Observations from 3 User Interactions with the Interface
Observation #1:
  • User 1 tried to turn the sprinklers on by pressing the ON button. However, after nothing happened, he pressed the MAN START/ADV button but still saw that nothing changed.
  • After pushing some of the other buttons, he spent around a minute reading all of the labels. He then twisted the turn dial to AUTO and was able to turn the sprinklers on by pressing MAN START/ADV again.
  • The user's approach was systematic. He pressed each button and turned the dial to each option to monitor how the screen would change.
  • He kept twisting the turn dial between Days to Water and Program Start Time to set a schedule for the sprinklers.
  • He was unable to schedule the sprinklers to turn on for a specific day. He gave up after 11 minutes.
Observation #2:
  • User 2 tried to turn the sprinklers on by pressing the ON button. However, after nothing happend, she pressed the A/B button.
  • She also spent around a minute reading all of the labels. She tried pressing MAN START/ADV and then immediately pressing ON.
  • She pressed the icon pictures.
  • She first turned the dial to Water Time per Station and then pressed ON. After nothing happend, she turned the dial to Saturday and pressed ON. This time, the sprinklers successfully turned on.
  • She pressed the OFF button to turn the spinklers off but nothing happend. Soon after, she pressed MAN START/ADV again and saw that the sprinklers changed to a different station. She continued to press it until the sprinklers turned off.
  • The user's approach was different in that she kept pressing the ON button and tried multiple ways to do so. This caused her to try different button combinations and to also try holding down the ON button.
  • After 13 minutes, she was successfully able to schedule the sprinklers, however, she said that she did not understand what the difference between A/B and the three program start times was.
Observation #3:
  • User 3 first checked the Day Cycle, which was something that nobody else did.
  • After checking the dial, he immediately turned the dial to AUTO and pressed MAN START/ADV and the sprinklers turned on.
  • To set the sprinklers for a schedule, he set the Program to B by pressing A/B, turned the dial to Tuesday and Saturday and pressed ON.
  • He turned the dial to 1 on Program Start Times and set the time for 7:00 AM.
  • He turned the dial to Auto, and then finished.
  • Unlike the previous users, this user commented that he has used this sprinkler system contoller for years. He was able to turn the sprinklers on and set a new schedule within two minutes.
Interview Questions and Summary
Questions:
  1. What do you expect the interface to allow you to do?
  2. What would motivate you to use this interface?
  3. Do you think this interface is intuitive? Why or why not?
  4. Did you face any challenges when using the interface? If so, what?
  5. On a scale of 1-10 (10 being the best and 1 being the worst), how effective would you say the interface was?
Summary:

The three users had drastically different levels of familiarity with the water sprinkler system, causing each user to interact with the system differently. Though each user believed that the water sprinkler controller would allow them to turn the sprinklers on and off and to set a specific time to do so, their motivation for why they would use the system varied. Most notably, the first two users simply said that they would use the system to water their plants. However, the third user, who was the most experienced, said that his main motivation is so that he does not forget to water his lawn, to water the lawn early in the morning to give the lawn enough time to absorb the water before the sun comes out, and to prevent fungus from growing.

Interestingly, although the first user was unsuccessful in scheduling the water system, he believed that the interface was fairly intuitive because of the clear labeling and rated it a 6/10. In contrast, the second and third users were adamant that the interface was the worst they had ever used, and both rated it a 1/10. All of the users agreed that the most unintuitive facet of the interface was using the turn dial, found the screen to be difficult to read, and believed that a manual would be necessary for most users.

2 Personas as Empathy Maps

Confident Calvin is an avid gardener and loves to spend time working outdoors to make his house look nicer. Like he has done so often, he is adjusting the sprinklers for the upcoming week and has no issues doing so. Confident Calvin represents users who may have once struggled to understand the interface but have learned how to use it efficently.

Sporadic Sophie has just moved into her new house and is a first-time user of the water sprinkler controller. Since the system does not come with a manual, Sophie does not understand what the labels mean and how to schedule the sprinklers for specific days. Sporadic Sophie represents first-time users who struggle to understand how the turn dial and buttons work in tandem and try to learn how to use the interface through trial and error.

Sporadic Sophie's Experience from Start to Finish

1. After just moving into her new house, Sporadic Sophie notices that her lawn needs to be watered.

2. She decides that it is time to set up her sprinkler system, so she reads the interface and presses the ON button to test the sprinklers.

3. After noticing that nothing happens, Sophie frantically presses other buttons at random and finds that the sprinklers turn on after she turns the dial to AUTO and presses MAN START/ADV.

4. Sporadic Sophie then decides that she wants the sprinklers to run on a schedule, so she tries setting the program to run on Thursdays.

5. Sophie tries to set the Program Start Time but is confused with the difference between the Program Start Time and the A/B programs.

6. She reads over the labels on the interface again to see if there are some details she has overlooked.

7. She feels frustrated and self-conscious because she struggles to understand how the different program start times and program types all work together, but turns to Confident Calvin for help after taking 15 minutes.

8. After Calvin tells her that Program A and B are different schedules and that the program start times can be used to water her lawn three times in a single day, Sophie understands and sets the sprinklers to run once on Thursdays.

Insights and Project Takeaways

This project sought to objectively observe three users using a water sprinkler controller to uncover what contributes to a simple yet effective interface. Through interviews and observations, is is apparent that an interface that uses two distinct methods of setting a user's preferences (e.g. having both buttons and a turn dial) hampers the interface's learnability.

Another interesting insight is that while most users agreed that the interface was well-labeled, they still struggled to understand the difference between the A/B programs and the three program start times. This may have been caused by A/B being a button and the program start times being listed as an option on the turn dial. Clearer descriptions are needed to help new users who may not know that some users water their lawn multiple times a day. Thus, an important takeaway is that overly complicated interfaces with too many options may overwhelm and frustrate new users.
